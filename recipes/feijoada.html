Feijoada

Description

Feijoada is a hearty Brazilian stew made with black beans, a variety of pork cuts, and flavorful spices. Traditionally served on special occasions, it's a dish that brings people together, often accompanied by rice, collard greens, and orange slices. This rich, comforting meal is perfect for sharing with family and friends and embodies the essence of Brazilian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 500g black beans
  • 500g pork shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 200g chorizo or smoked sausage, sliced
  • 200g bacon, diced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 4 cups water or chicken broth
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
  • Rice (for serving)
  • Collard greens (for serving, optional)
  • Orange slices (for serving, optional)

Steps

  1. Soak the black beans in water overnight, then drain them before cooking.
  2. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the bacon and cook until crispy, then remove and set aside.
  3. Add the pork shoulder and sausage to the pot and cook until browned on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  4. In the same pot, saut√© the onion and garlic until softened, about 5 minutes.
  5. Add the soaked black beans, bay leaves, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper to the pot. Stir to combine.
  6. Return the cooked bacon, pork shoulder, and sausage to the pot. Add the water or chicken broth and bring to a boil.
  7. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 2-3 hours, or until the beans are tender and the meat is cooked through, adding more water if necessary to maintain the desired consistency.
  8. Serve the Feijoada with rice, collard greens, and orange slices. Garnish with fresh cilantro.
