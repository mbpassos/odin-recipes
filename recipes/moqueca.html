Moqueca

Description

Moqueca is a traditional Brazilian fish stew that combines the vibrant flavors of coconut milk, tomatoes, and aromatic herbs. This dish is often made with fish like tilapia or snapper, and is typically served with rice. The rich, fragrant broth and tender fish make Moqueca a comforting and flavorful meal, perfect for sharing with friends and family.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg firm white fish fillets (such as tilapia or snapper)
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 400ml coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon palm oil (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Marinate the fish fillets with lime juice, garlic, salt, and pepper for at least 30 minutes.
  2. In a large pot, heat the olive oil (and palm oil, if using) over medium heat. Add the onion and bell pepper, and saut√© until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the chopped tomatoes to the pot and cook for another 2 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Pour in the coconut milk and bring the mixture to a simmer. Stir in the paprika and some additional salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Gently place the marinated fish fillets into the pot. Cover and cook for 10-15 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.
  6. Sprinkle fresh cilantro over the top of the Moqueca and serve immediately with rice.
